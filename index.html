Introducing the Dataset:


The nonprofit Open Sourcing Mental Illness (OSMI) conducted an annual survey from 2016 to 2019, in which tech professionals voluntarily answered questions about their experience with mental health in the tech industry.

Over these four years, 2,958 people took the survey.

The following visualizations examine OSMI's survey results, and what the results say about mental health in tech.

According to a study done by the World Health Organization, a negative workplace environment can lead to physical & mental health problems, loss of productivity, and absenteeism.

An estimated $1 trillion a year of productivity is lost due to depression and anxiety disorders.

This can be attributed to a variety of poor workplace practices, including bad management practices, low levels of support for employees, inflexible working hours, and inadequate health policies.

The Future of Mental Health in Tech
The future of mental health in the tech industry is reliant on proactivity today. OSMI's survey responses reveal three main takeaways for how to move forward:

Support underrepresented demographics.
Survey data shows that women , non-binary genders , and Hispanic/Black tech professionals struggle the most with mental health issues.

Educate management.
Survey data shows that many tech professionals who are struggling with mental health issues work in environments where employers do not provide enough support for employee mental health.

Hold employees accountable.
Survey data shows that tech professionals' coworkers often dismissed, stigmatized, and mocked mental health issues. More should be formally done to incentivize a supportive environment.
A stronger, more supportive work culture is everyone's responsibility.
