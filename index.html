Over the past 20 years, the tech industry has become one of the fastest growing sectors in the US Economy.
As a result of the tech boom, jobs in tech have become extremely desirable, advertising high salaries, job stability, and a fast-paced work environment...

...But is a job in tech really all that it seems?

Introducing the Dataset:


The nonprofit Open Sourcing Mental Illness (OSMI) conducted an annual survey from 2016 to 2019, in which tech professionals voluntarily answered questions about their experience with mental health in the tech industry.

Over these four years, 2,958 people took the survey.

The following visualizations examine OSMI's survey results, and what the results say about mental health in tech.

Individual Stories

Is mental health in the tech industry getting better over time?
The trajectory for the future of mental health in the tech industry is still unclear.

The data reveals these key takeaways:

1. Support Underrepresented Minorities
The data reveals that a significant percentage of tech professionals across all demographics are struggling with mental health disorders.But minority demographics are struggling the most.
2. Educate management
The data reveals that many tech professionals who feel that the tech industry is poorly handling mental health work in environments where employers never discuss or provide resources for employees' mental health.
3. Hold employees accountable
Many personal anecdotes revealed that both tech professionals' supervisors and coworkers often dismissed, stigmatized, and mocked mental health issues. More should be formally done to incentivize a supportive work environment.
A stronger, more supportive work culture is everyone's responsibility.
